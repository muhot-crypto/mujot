🔍 Find Your Topic ~10 min
Where to look. What to look for. How to know it's worth making.

Reddit
Go to r/ChatGPT, r/artificial, r/OutOfTheLoop. Top posts = what normal people are confused about.
free
YouTube Search
Search an AI topic. Sort by "This week." Low view counts on recent videos = underserved topic.
free
Step-by-step
  • Open Google Trends → search an AI term you heard recently → set to United States / Past 7 days
  • If the graph is rising or spiking, the topic is alive. If it's flat, skip it.
  • Cross-check on Reddit: search the same topic in r/ChatGPT. If people are asking questions about it, those ARE your video angles.
  • Pick ONE topic. Write it as a single plain question a non-tech person would ask. Example: "Why does ChatGPT sometimes just make things up?"
  • That question = your video title and your script goal.
02
🧠 Research It Simply ~10 min
Get the facts fast. No rabbit holes. Just what you need to explain it clearly.
▶ tap to expand
Perplexity AI
Ask your question. It gives sourced answers in plain language. Much faster than Googling.
free / pro
Claude / ChatGPT
Use to simplify what Perplexity gives you. Ask "explain this like I'm 15 years old."
free / pro
Step-by-step
  • Go to perplexity.ai → paste your question exactly as a curious person would ask it
  • Read the answer. Highlight or copy the 3–5 most interesting facts or moments
  • If something is still confusing, paste THAT sentence into Claude and ask it to simplify
  • You don't need to understand every technical detail — you need to understand it well enough to explain the why and the so-what
  • Save your simplified notes in one place (Notes app, Notion, anywhere)
Perplexity prompt template
Explain [TOPIC] in simple terms. What is it, why do people care about it, and how does it affect regular people's lives? Keep it factual and easy to understand. No jargon.

Script structure — every video
  • Hook (0–3 sec): One shocking or curious statement. No intro. No "hey guys." Just the hook.
  • Relate (3–10 sec): Connect it to something the viewer already knows or experiences
  • Explain (10–40 sec): The actual simple explanation. One idea at a time.
  • Real example (40–55 sec): Show it happening in real life. Concrete. Specific.
  • Takeaway (55–60 sec): What should they do, think, or feel differently now?
  • For each line, write a VISUAL NOTE next to it — what should be on screen when this is said
Claude script prompt — save and reuse this
You are a scriptwriter for a faceless educational TikTok/YouTube channel targeting everyday Americans with no tech background. Write a 60-second script about [TOPIC] using these notes: [PASTE YOUR RESEARCH]. Format it as two columns: VOICEOVER and VISUAL NOTE (what should appear on screen). Tone: curious friend explaining something over coffee. No jargon. Start with a hook — a surprising or relatable statement, no intro. End with a clear takeaway.

🎙️ Generate Voiceover ~5 min
Pick a consistent US voice. Paste script. Download audio. Done.
▶ tap to expand
ElevenLabs
Best quality. Pick a US voice. "Rachel" or "Adam" work well for documentary tone. Sounds very natural.
recommended
Murf AI
More narrator-style voices. Great for a "How It's Made" documentary feel.
alternative
Step-by-step
  • Go to elevenlabs.io → Speech Synthesis
  • Paste ONLY the voiceover column from your script (not the visual notes)
  • Pick your voice. Stick to the same voice every video — it becomes your brand signature
  • Set stability to ~50%, similarity to ~75%. Generate.
  • Listen once. If a word sounds wrong, manually retype it phonetically (e.g. "GIF" → "JIF")
  • Download as MP3. Label it VOICEOVER_[topic]_[date].mp3
05
🎬 Gather Visuals ~25 min
Match every visual note from your script to a clip or image. Three sources, in order of preference.
▶ tap to expand
Storyblocks
Flat monthly fee, unlimited stock footage. Search everyday scenes: people on phones, offices, doctors, grocery stores.
first choice
Pexels / Pixabay
100% free stock footage. Not as wide a library but covers most basics. Always try here first before paying.
free
Runway ML
Generate short AI video clips for abstract things stock footage can't cover. "Data flowing through a network", etc.
for abstract
Canva AI
Generate simple infographics, diagrams, title cards. Very fast. Good for "stats" or "timeline" visuals.
for graphics
Step-by-step
  • Open your script. Go line by line through the VISUAL NOTE column
  • For each note: search Pexels first (free). If nothing fits, try Storyblocks.
  • For abstract tech concepts (AI, data, algorithms): use Runway to generate a 3–5 second clip with a text prompt
  • For any "statistic" or "comparison" moment: open Canva, use their AI to make a quick graphic
  • Download everything into one folder labeled VISUALS_[topic]
  • You need roughly one clip per 5–8 seconds of voiceover

Assembly order in CapCut
  • Import voiceover MP3 first — it becomes your timeline backbone
  • Drag video clips onto the timeline aligned to what's being said at that moment
  • Use Auto Captions — CapCut generates them in seconds. Edit any wrong words.
  • Add background music at 5–10% volume — just enough to fill silence. Never louder than the voice.
  • Add a simple text title card at the very start (0.5 seconds) — one line, big font, the hook in text form
  • Export at 1080p, 60fps for TikTok. YouTube Shorts same. For full YouTube: 1080p minimum, 4K if possible.

Upload checklist
  • Title: Paste your hook question into VidIQ. Pick the version with highest search volume. Keep it under 60 characters.
  • Thumbnail: One image, one bold text line (4 words max), high contrast. Test: can you read it at thumbnail size?
  • Description: First 2 lines matter most (shown before "read more"). Put your key phrase there. Ask Claude to write it.
  • Tags / Hashtags: TikTok: 3–5 hashtags max. YouTube: 8–10 relevant tags. Use VidIQ suggestions.
  • Schedule: Upload to Buffer → set to 7:00pm EST for TikTok, 12:00pm EST for YouTube
  • Make sure NordVPN is connected to your US server before opening any of these apps
Claude description prompt
Write a YouTube video description for a video titled "[YOUR TITLE]". The audience is everyday Americans with no tech background. First 2 lines should hook them and include the main keyword. Then a 3-sentence summary. Keep total length under 150 words. No emojis.
